            <p>Food provides the energy that our bodies need to keep going. Without food, a person would typically survive for a few weeks. Without water, they could only live for a few days.</p>

  <section id="Carbohydrates" class="main-section">
    <header>Carbohydrates</header>
    <p>What are carbohydrates?</p>
    <p> Carbohydrates, or carbs, are sugar molecules. Along with proteins and fats, carbohydrates are one of three main nutrients found in foods and drinks. Your body breaks down carbohydrates into glucose. Glucose, or blood sugar, is the main source of energy for your body's cells, tissues, and organs.</p>
    <code>Examples are:</code>
    <ul>
      <li>Breads</li>
      <li>wheat</li>
      <li>Rice</li>
      <li>corn</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
  <section id="Fat_and_oil" class="main-section">
    <header>Fat and oil</header>
    <p>What is Fat and oil.</p>
    <p>Some people think that fats and oils are different things. But in reality, they share a common chemical structure and similar properties. The difference is that those that stay solid at room temperatures are called fats while those that stay liquid at room temperatures are called oils</p>
    <p>what then is now Fat?</p>
    <p> Fats are composed of high amounts of saturated fatty acids, which at room temperature can take a solid form.</p>
    <p>what then is now oil?</p>
    <p>On the other hand, Oils are composed of unsaturated acids, which at room temperature can take a liquid form.</p>
    <code>Examples of both are:</code>
    <ul>
      <li>goose fat</li>
      <li>Butter</li>
      <li>palm oil</li>
      <li>coconut oil </li>
    </ul>
  </section>
  <section id="Protein" class="main-section">
    <header>Protein</header>
    <p>What is Protein</p>
    <p>Proteins are made up of building blocks called amino acids. There are about 20 different amino acids that link together in different combinations. Your body uses them to make new proteins, such as muscle and bone, and other compounds such as enzymes and hormones. It can also use them as an energy source.</p>
    <p>Protein is a macronutrient that is essential for the growth and repair of tissues in the body. It is made up of amino acids and can be found in a variety of foods </p>
    <code>Examples are:</code>
    <ul>
      <li>meat</li>
      <li>fish</li>
      <li>eggs</li>
      <li>diary(milk)</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
  <section id="Vitamins" class="main-section">
    <header>Vitamins</header>
    <p>What are Vitamins</p>
    <p>Vitamins and minerals are micronutrients required by the body to carry out a range of normal functions. However, these micronutrients are not produced in our bodies and must be derived from the food we eat. Vitamins are organic substances that are generally classified as either fat soluble or water soluble.</p>
    <code>Examples are:</code>
    <ul>
      <li>iron — meat, poultry, fish, and beans.
  </li>
      <li>vitamin A — carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, kale.</li>
      <li>vitamin B12 — meat, poultry, fish.</li>
      <li>vitamin E — nuts, seeds, vegetable oils.</li>
    </ul>
  </section>
  <section id="Mineral" class="main-section">
    <header>Mineral</header>
    <p>What are Mineral</p>
    <p>A mineral nutrient refers to certain chemical elements found in food that play important roles in the proper functioning of the human body</p>
    <code>Examples are:</code>
    <ul>
      <li>calcium</li>
      <li>phosphorus</li>
      <li>potassium</li>
      <li>sodium</li>
    </ul>

    <p>What is water</p>
    <p>water, a substance composed of the chemical elements hydrogen and oxygen and existing in gaseous, liquid, and solid states. It is one of the most plentiful and essential of compounds. A tasteless and odourless liquid at room temperature, it has the important ability to dissolve many other substances.</p>
